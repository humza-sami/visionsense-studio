02 · How it works

The pipeline — 15 cameras feed one GPU

The core design decision: never one model per camera. Every stream flows into a single batched GPU pipeline. Click any stage for detail.

Click a stage above to see what it does.

Why batching matters: one inference call over a [15 × 3 × 640 × 640] tensor keeps the tensor cores busy once, instead of 15 small calls fighting for the GPU. The decoder chip (NVDEC) handles all 15 H.265 streams at just ~7% utilisation — video decode never touches the CPU.

05 · Summary

What to sell, and at what capacity

YOLO26-nano
30 cams max · 25–28 safe
The volume play. Full 25 fps tracking on every feed; detection 3.7–9 fps/cam even with every-frame testing. Best PKR-per-camera: 5,000 PKR/cam at 30.
YOLO26-large
20 cams max · 15 sweet spot
The accuracy/capacity balance — noticeably better small & distant object detection. 15 cameras at 5.3 det-fps with 1.5 GB VRAM spare. 7,500–10,000 PKR/cam.
YOLO26-xlarge
10 cams max on 8 GB
Maximum accuracy, premium tier. 15 feeds only via chunked batch-5 engine at ~1 det-fps — recommend a bigger-VRAM card for x-large at scale.
  • VRAM is the wall before compute in the nano/large sweeps. At the 30-camera nano limit, GPU compute averaged 24.8% and NVDEC 13.7%, but VRAM already peaked at 7.5 GB. Camera capacity scales with VRAM, so a 16 GB card ≈ 2× the cameras, a 24 GB card ≈ 3×, before CPU becomes the next limit.
  • Every feed always captures at the full 25 fps. Detection fps is the per-frame AI rate; on-screen tracking stays at 25 fps because ByteTrack carries IDs between detections. The x-large batch-5 live run confirms all 15 streams remain visible, while nano/large remain the practical production tiers.
  • Decode is free. One NVDEC chip handled 30 H.265 streams at ≤14% — the same silicon could decode 100+ camera substreams. H.265 substreams (704×576) are the right AI input; keep main streams for recording.
  • Cost per camera today: 150,000 PKR ÷ 15–30 cameras = 5,000–10,000 PKR per camera, one-time hardware cost, all-inclusive on-prem (no cloud fees, no per-frame API costs).
  • The detection-fps drop with camera count is software, not silicon — a single Python loop doing preview/color-convert for every camera. GPU-side color convert and decoupled preview raise both the fps and the camera ceiling on identical hardware.
Recommended default offer: YOLO26-large · 15 cameras · 150K PKR box = accurate all-class detection at 10,000 PKR/camera with 25 fps live tracking, room to upsell nano-based 25–30 camera volume deals on the same hardware.
Method.
Feeds: one 704×576 H.265 25 fps clip published to a local MediaMTX RTSP relay and fanned out to N identical streams (mirrors an NVR serving N substreams) · Decode: GStreamer nvh265dec (NVDEC hardware) · Inference: single shared TensorRT engine, dynamic batch, one batched call per tick · Fair-test settings: det_interval=1 (every frame), all 80 COCO classes, conf 0.25, uncapped loop, no per-camera limits · Each camera count runs in a fresh process (clean VRAM), 15 s warmup + 30 s steady-state sampling via nvidia-smi and /proc/stat · Reproduce: python scripts/benchmark_csv.py sweep "5,10,15,20,25,30" 30 1 640 0.25 rtsp://127.0.0.1:8554/live · Accuracy column in the CSV is reserved for manual review annotations.
